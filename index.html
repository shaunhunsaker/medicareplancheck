Frequently Asked Questions

When can I enroll in a Medicare Advantage plan?

The main enrollment periods are: Initial Enrollment Period (the 7-month window around your 65th birthday), Annual Enrollment Period (Oct 15 – Dec 7 each year), and Medicare Advantage Open Enrollment (Jan 1 – Mar 31). Special Enrollment Periods may also apply if you have a qualifying life event. Call us and we’ll confirm exactly when you can enroll.

Is there really a $0 premium Medicare Advantage plan?

Yes — many Medicare Advantage plans have $0 monthly premiums. You still pay your Medicare Part B premium (typically $202.90/month in 2026), but the Advantage plan itself charges nothing extra. Availability depends on your zip code and the carriers in your area. Our agents can quickly tell you what $0 plans are available near you.

What’s the difference between Medicare Advantage and Medicare Supplement?

Medicare Advantage replaces Original Medicare and often includes extra benefits (dental, vision, drugs) at low or no premium, but uses a network of doctors. Medicare Supplement supplements Original Medicare, covering your out-of-pocket costs — giving you more flexibility to use any doctor nationwide but typically at a higher monthly premium. The best choice depends on your health needs, budget, and how often you travel or see specialists.

Do I need to switch plans every year?

You don’t have to, but it’s smart to review your plan every year during the Annual Enrollment Period (Oct 15 – Dec 7). Premiums, benefits, and drug formularies change annually. A quick call with our agents can confirm whether your current plan is still your best option or if a better one is available.

How much does it cost to use your service?

Absolutely nothing. Our service is completely free to Medicare beneficiaries. Licensed insurance agents are compensated by the insurance carriers when you enroll in a plan — that cost is never passed on to you.
